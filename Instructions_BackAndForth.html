Overview

In this survey, your task is to estimate whether several hypothetical persons make true or false statements.
Each estimation task has the following structure, which will be explained in greater detail on the next screens.

  • Before a person is presented to you, the computer randomly determines whether the person is reliable or unreliable.
    The probability for each type is 50%.
  • After it is randomly determined whether the person is reliable or unreliable, the person makes one or several statements.
  • An independent fact checker then potentially checks each statement.
    {% if player.check_order == "high_low" %} Each statement is checked with a probability of 75%.
    • {% endif %} {% if player.check_order == "low_high" %} Each statement is checked with a probability of 25%. {% endif %}

Your bonus payment in this survey depends partly on chance and partly on how close your estimates are to the correct answers.

Therefore, please read the following instructions carefully!

Reliable and Unreliable Persons

Before a person is presented to you, the computer randomly determines whether the person is reliable or unreliable.
The probability for each type is 50%.

Statements by reliable persons are more likely to be correct than statements by unreliable persons.

In particular:

  • A statement by a Reliable Person is true with a probability of {{ Constants.Type2_true }}.
    Note that this does not mean that exactly eight out of ten statements must be correct!
    Rather, each single statement is true with a probability of {{ Constants.Type2_true }}.
  • A statement by an Unreliable Person is true with a probability of {{ Constants.Type1_true }}.
    Note that this does not mean that exactly two out of ten statements must be correct!
    Rather, each single statement is true with a probability of {{ Constants.Type1_true }}.

NOTE: A person's type is never displayed to you. The information from the fact checker,
which we explain in detail on the next screen, may help you to guess whether a person is more likely to be reliable or unreliable!

Fact checker

Each statement is checked with a probability of 75% by the fact checker.
Note that this does not mean that exactly seven and a half out of ten statements are checked!
Rather, each single statement is checked with a probability of 75%.

{% endif %} {% if player.check_order == "low_high" %}

Each statement is checked with a probability of 25% by the fact checker.
Note that this does not mean that exactly two and a half out of ten statements are checked!
Rather, each single statement is checked with a probability of 25%.

{% endif %}

The fact checker is always correct. There are hence three types of statements:

  • If the fact checker checks a statement and detects that it is false, the statement is labelled with:
  • If the fact checker checks a statement and detects that it is correct, there is NO label.
  • If the fact checker does not check a statement (and the statement is either true or false),
    there is NO label either.

Please note the following two implications:

  • Whenever you see the label next to a statement, you know it must be false.
    In other words, the probability that such a statement is correct is 0%.
  • Whenever you see no label next to a statement, you cannot know for sure whether the statement was checked
    and is true, or the statement was not checked and can either be true or false.

  1. The computer randomly determines whether the person is reliable or unreliable.

  2. The person makes one or several statements.

  3. {% if player.check_order == "high_low" %} With a probability of 75%, each statement from a person is checked by the independent fact checker. {% endif %} {% if player.check_order == "low_high" %} With a probability of 25%, each statement from a person is checked by the independent fact checker. {% endif %}
    • The fact checker is always right.
    • If the fact checker checked a statement and the statement is false, the label is attached to the statement.
    • No labels appear at statements that have not been checked or that have been checked and are true.

Bonus payment

After the survey, we will randomly select several participants.
These participants have the chance to earn a bonus payment of 10 USD.
The selected participants will receive the payment via their Prolific account.

Of the selected participants, we will randomly draw one estimation.
The chance to get the bonus payment depends on this estimation.

The closer the estimation is to the truth, the higher the chance for the bonus payment.
Note: The "truth" is equal to 0 if the statement is false, and equal to 100 if the statement is correct.

You will receive further details on your potential bonus payment at the end of the survey.
